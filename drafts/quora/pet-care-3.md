Busy households have a specific problem: the person who usually handles pet stuff isn't always the one home at feeding time or pill time. Information needs to be somewhere everyone can see it.

My family went through this when we had two dogs on different medications and my wife and I kept accidentally double-feeding or missing doses because neither of us knew what the other had done. Leaving notes on the counter worked sometimes, but notes get buried under mail or thrown away by mistake.

What helped was making the system visible and shareable. We needed something both of us could check from our phones, and it had to show what was done today, not what was scheduled. The question "did anyone feed the dog yet?" shouldn't require a text conversation every morning.

For medications specifically, set alarms but don't rely only on alarms. You need a way to confirm the dose was given. This is where I ended up building the Pet Care Organizer App at Clearfolks — I wanted to log that something happened, not be reminded it should happen.

Feeding schedules get complicated when you have pets with dietary restrictions or weight management needs. We found it helpful to measure portions in advance and keep them in labeled containers. That way anyone in the house can handle feeding without guessing amounts.

The bigger picture advice: assign one person as the "pet admin" who keeps the system updated, but make sure everyone can see and use it. You don't want knowledge trapped in one person's head. When that person travels for work or gets sick, the whole system falls apart.

Keep it boring and consistent. Pets thrive on routine, and so do the humans trying to care for them.
