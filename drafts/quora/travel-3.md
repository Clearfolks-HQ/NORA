The honest answer is that most people don't keep everything in one place until they've been burned by not doing it.

I spent years with flight confirmations in email, hotel bookings in a different email thread, packing lists in Apple Notes, and the rental car confirmation as a screenshot somewhere in my camera roll. It worked fine until it didn't — specifically, standing at a hotel desk in Barcelona with a dead phone battery and no idea what my booking reference was.

Start with a single document or app and commit to it. Every confirmation email gets its key details copied there: confirmation number, address, check-in time, contact phone number. Yes, this takes five minutes per booking. It saves an hour of panic later.

For packing lists, I keep a master template that I copy and modify per trip. Tropical vacation gets the reef-safe sunscreen added. Business trip gets the blazer. Winter trip gets the thermal base layers. Starting from scratch each time means you'll forget something obvious.

The document storage question is trickier. I photograph every passport, ID, and insurance card, then keep digital copies separate from my phone — cloud storage that family members can access if something happens to me. After dealing with exactly this problem too many times, I built a Travel Planner App at Clearfolks that holds itinerary, bookings, packing list and budget in one offline-accessible place.

Physical organization matters too. I use a single folder for printed confirmations and keep passport, boarding passes, and hotel confirmation together in one pocket. The goal is never asking "which bag has the..."

The system matters less than consistency. Whatever method you choose, use it for every single trip detail from the moment you start planning.
