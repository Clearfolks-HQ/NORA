These three categories — utilities, address changes, and packing — feel separate but they're deeply connected by timing. Miss one, and it creates cascading problems.

Start with utilities because they have the longest lead times. Call your current providers at least two weeks before the move to schedule disconnection for the day after you leave, not the day of. Then contact providers at the new address for connection the day before you arrive. Nothing worse than showing up to a house with no running water.

For address changes, make a master list of everyone who sends you mail. Most people remember the post office, DMV, and banks but forget subscriptions, medical providers, insurance companies, and that one uncle who still sends birthday cards. The post office forwarding service catches some of this, but not everything.

Packing is where most people start, but it should come last in your planning. Know your utility and paperwork deadlines first, then build packing around that schedule. We learned to pack room by room rather than category by category. Mixing kitchen items with bathroom items sounds efficient until you're searching through 40 boxes for a can opener.

After our second move descended into chaos with important dates slipping through cracks, I built the Moving Day Organizer App specifically to track packing room by room, utility transfers, address changes, and the overall timeline to the move. It solved the problem of having critical information scattered across three apps and a paper notebook.

Keep one folder — physical or digital — with confirmation numbers for every utility transfer and address change. When something goes wrong at the new place, and something always does, you'll need that documentation quickly.
