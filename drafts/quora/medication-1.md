When my mother-in-law moved in with us three years ago, she had seven different prescriptions from three different doctors. The first month was chaos. We missed a refill because we didn't realize she was almost out. We double-dosed her blood pressure medication once because my wife and I both thought the other one hadn't given it to her yet. It was stressful for everyone.

What actually worked for us came down to three things. First, we created a single source of truth. It doesn't matter if it's a spreadsheet, a notebook, or an app — what matters is that everyone in the household knows where to look and where to record. Second, we noted which doctor prescribed what. This sounds obvious until you're on the phone with a pharmacy at 6pm trying to figure out who needs to authorize a refill. Third, we tracked not doses but also how she was feeling. Some side effects crept up slowly, and having notes helped her cardiologist adjust things faster.

I ended up building Medication Tracker at Clearfolks specifically because the tools I found were either way too complicated or didn't let me connect prescriptions to the doctors behind them, which turned out to be the thing I needed most.

The practical advice: start simple. Write down every medication, the dose, when it's taken, when it was last refilled, and who prescribed it. Put that information somewhere everyone involved can access. If you're coordinating with siblings or a caregiver, make sure they can see updates too. And pay attention to the refill dates — setting reminders a week before you expect to run out gives you breathing room to deal with insurance or pharmacy issues.

The goal isn't perfection. It's making sure nothing important slips through the cracks.
