My wife and I planned our wedding ourselves about eight years ago, and the biggest lesson I took away was that the "best" app isn't the one with the most features — it's the one both of you will actually open.

We tried a few popular options and found they were either built for professional planners with way more complexity than we needed, or they were basically glorified checklists that didn't let us work together in real time. The sweet spot is something lightweight that handles the four things that actually matter: what you're spending, who's coming, who you're paying, and when things need to happen.

The couples I've talked to who felt calm throughout planning had one thing in common — a single shared source of truth. Not a Google Doc here, a spreadsheet there, and texts flying back and forth. One place where either person could check the status of something without asking the other.

That's actually why I built our Wedding Planning App at Clearfolks — it holds the budget, vendors, guest list and timeline in one shared place both partners can see, because that's exactly what we wished we'd had.

Beyond tools, my practical advice: assign clear ownership. One person handles catering communication, the other handles photographer. You both see everything, but you're not stepping on each other. Also, check in weekly for fifteen minutes. Not to stress, to sync. It keeps small problems from becoming big ones.

The couples who struggle most are the ones where one partner is "handling it" and the other is in the dark until something goes wrong. Shared visibility from day one changes everything.
