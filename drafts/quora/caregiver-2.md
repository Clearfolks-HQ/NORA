I spent years looking for something like this for my own family and came up mostly frustrated. The apps that existed either did way too much or not nearly enough.

Most medication reminder apps focus on the person taking the pills, not the family member trying to keep track from a distance. They'll ping you to take your Metformin but they won't help your daughter in another state know what happened at your cardiology appointment last week.

Calendar apps work fine for appointments but they're not designed to capture the context that matters. "Dr. Patel, Tuesday 2pm" tells you nothing about what questions you needed to ask or what changed at the last visit.

Contact managers don't understand that you need the pharmacy number, the insurance member ID, the home health aide's schedule, and your brother's work number all in one view when something goes wrong at 9pm.

What I really needed was something that treated the whole picture as one problem. Medications, appointments, the people involved, and the notes that explain why things are set up the way they are. I built Caregiver Command Center at Clearfolks after trying to cobble this together with spreadsheets and shared docs for two years.

The honest truth is that no app solves the hard parts of caregiving. The emotional weight, the difficult conversations, the guilt when you can't be there. But losing track of what medication got changed or missing that the physical therapist called should be a solved problem. Those are logistics failures, not caregiving failures, and logistics can be fixed with the right tool.

Whatever you choose, make sure everyone involved can access it easily and that it doesn't require a training session to update.
