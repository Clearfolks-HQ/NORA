Most pet owners I've talked to rely on some combination of paper notes, phone reminders, and memory. The problem is these systems work fine until they don't — you forget the heartworm pill was due three days ago, or you can't remember when the last rabies shot happened when the boarding kennel asks.

What actually works is having one place where everything lives. I've seen people use spreadsheets, notes apps, even dedicated notebooks. The key isn't the tool, it's the habit of recording things right when they happen. Write down the vet visit date and what was done before you leave the parking lot. Set the next reminder while you're still thinking about it.

For medications especially, you need a system that accounts for the fact that life gets hectic. A reminder that fires at the same time every day helps, but you also want to be able to mark when you actually gave the dose — not when it was scheduled. That gap matters when you're trying to figure out if you already gave the morning pill or not.

I built a Pet Care Organizer App at Clearfolks specifically because I kept losing track of my dog's flea treatment schedule and couldn't remember which year we'd done his dental cleaning.

Grooming is easier to let slide because nothing urgent happens if you miss a nail trim by a week. But keeping a simple log helps you notice patterns — like realizing your dog needs grooming more often in summer, or that the new food is working better than the old one.

The honest answer is that any system beats no system. Pick something you'll actually use, put everything in one spot, and check it when you're at the vet so you can answer their questions without guessing.
