The trick I learned after a few chaotic family trips is to work backwards from the moments that would ruin everything if they went wrong.

Start with the non-negotiables: flights, hotel check-in times, any reservations that have hard deadlines. Write those down first with exact times and confirmation numbers. Then layer in the stuff that matters to your specific family. If your kid has food allergies, restaurant research goes on the list early. If someone needs medication, pharmacy locations near your hotel get added.

I keep a running notes file for each trip that starts weeks before we leave. Every time I think "oh, I should remember to pack that" or "we need to book that activity," it goes in the file immediately. The alternative is lying awake at 2am three days before departure trying to remember what you forgot.

For the actual packing, I make separate lists by person and by bag. Sounds excessive until you're standing at the airport realizing your daughter's inhaler is in a checked bag. After dealing with scattered confirmations and forgotten chargers one too many times, I built a Travel Planner App at Clearfolks that holds the itinerary, bookings, packing list and budget in one place I can pull up offline — because airport wifi is never reliable when you need to show a confirmation.

The budget piece matters more than people think. I track estimated costs before the trip and actual spending during. It prevents that sinking feeling when you check your credit card statement afterward.

One last thing: share access with your travel partner. Nothing worse than being the only person who knows where the rental car confirmation is while you're stuck in a customs line.
