The key is making logging so fast and automatic that it doesn't feel like a task. Because at 2am when you've been up three times already, anything that requires more than a few seconds becomes another thing you resent doing.

My wife and I stumbled through our first few weeks trying to remember everything, then switched to pen and paper, then tried apps that wanted too much information. What finally worked was stripping down to the minimum: when feeding started, which side or how many ounces, when they woke up, when they fell asleep, wet or dirty diaper. That's it.

The real trick is having a system both of you can update. Nothing burns you out faster than being the only one who knows when the baby last ate. If you're trading shifts, you need to glance at a log and know what happened without a verbal handoff. Sleep is too precious to spend it debriefing your partner.

A few things that helped us: put the logging tool where you'll actually use it. For us that was phones propped on the nightstand and the changing table. Lower the bar for what counts as logged — rough times are fine, you don't need precision. And don't try to track everything. Pick the three or four things that actually matter for knowing if the baby's okay and making feeding or doctor conversations easier.

I built the Clearfolks Baby Tracker and Postpartum App specifically because we needed something we could update with one hand that wouldn't overwhelm us with charts and features we'd never use.

Accept now that some feeds won't get logged. Some diapers will slip by. That's fine. You're going for "good enough to spot patterns and answer the pediatrician's questions," not perfection.
